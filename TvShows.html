Queen Sugar

Queen Sugar is a television drama series created by Ava DuVernay, which follows the lives of three siblings in Louisiana who inherit an 800-acre sugarcane farm from their recently deceased father. Charley, the eldest sibling, is a savvy businesswoman who moves from Los Angeles to help manage the farm and struggles to adapt to life in the rural South. Nova, the middle sibling, is a journalist and activist who works to uncover social injustices in her community, while the youngest sibling, Ralph Angel, is an ex-convict who seeks redemption and tries to prove himself as a responsible father to his young son. As they navigate the challenges of running a family business, they also confront their own personal struggles and face the complexities of their relationships with each other and the wider community. The show explores themes of race, class, family, and identity, and is known for its richly drawn characters and powerful storytelling.
